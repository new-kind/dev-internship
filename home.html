> Design

  1. While often thought of as sequential, I interpret design and development as an integrated system where one can not function without constant dependency on the other. When a developer publishes content, they must consider how the user or end group will interact with their data. This includes how the user will read the content, how they will navigate through the content, and even how they will store and utilize the content in the future. If there is good design, the content will be easy to navigate and recall for later use, but if the design is lacking it can hinder ease of use, and even prevent access to some developed features. A developer's role in a design setting is to deliver clean and understandable content that is easily manipulated so that further on in the design process it is easy to optimize for a specific platform or goal.

  2. Grids are useful to easily place and space multiple sections or panels of content on a webpage. A proper grid design can offer flexibility and compatibility across many screen sizes, resolutions, and platforms. When I go about creating a grid, I nest div containers and then float the inside containers either to the left or right depending on how I need the content. I often include a header and footer element on the top and bottom of a main element which holds the most important information of that webpage. I utilize borders and padding to appropriately space my div containers, and percentages to proportionally create div container sizes when necessary.

  3. Both pattern libraries and visual systems are ways of keeping web content consistent and unique. They offer repeatable components such as carousels, layouts, colors, typography, etc. These are extremely useful because it allows a large team of designers and developers to pull pieces from a common location, without having to waste time and recreate that same element from scratch. This also allows future changes and new pieces to remain consistent, and new pieces added over time can be shared in the library or system to ensure consistency. I have not personally built a pattern library or visual system but I have worked with frameworks such as Bootstrap and Materialize that include these libraries and systems.
